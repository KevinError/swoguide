What to Bring to Your First Ship

Pack for the reporting process first. Let your sponsor and command guidance decide what comes next.

Reviewed

July 20, 2026

Use with

Current command guidance and official references

The rule: confirm before you buy

Your sponsor and reporting instructions know your ship, location, schedule, and immediate requirements better than a generic checklist. Ask what uniform you will report in, which items are hard to obtain locally, what storage is realistically available, and whether you should expect to move aboard immediately.

Avoid buying specialized gear because someone online called it essential. Uniform standards and command policies change, and storage aboard ship is limited. Bring the required basics, then adjust after you understand your command.

Keep these accessible

  • Orders and any endorsements or amendments required for reporting.
  • Military identification and other identification needed for travel or access.
  • Travel receipts and documents you must retain for your claim.
  • Required medical, training, or administrative records identified by your command.
  • Your sponsor’s and quarterdeck’s verified contact information stored offline.
  • A pen, a compact notebook, and any prescribed medication in original packaging.
Protect personal information

Carry sensitive documents securely. Do not photograph, upload, or email records through unapproved services merely for convenience.

Build a conservative first bag

Pack a complete, inspection-ready set of required uniforms and enough ordinary personal items to bridge your first several days. Label belongings discreetly according to command guidance. Choose compact, durable items and leave room for equipment you may be issued later.

  • Required uniforms, covers, shoes, insignia, and basic care supplies.
  • Modest toiletries in a compact bag and quick-drying shower items.
  • A small laundry bag and a few sealable organizers.
  • A simple, non-smart backup watch if permitted and useful.
  • Charging cables and a compact power bank used only where authorized.
  • Ear protection or other PPE only as issued or approved by your command.

What to leave behind at first

Large luggage, expensive electronics, bulky bedding, tools, and personally selected protective equipment can become problems when space and policy are unknown. Do not bring weapons, prohibited items, unauthorized recording equipment, or anything your command has restricted.

After your first week, make a second list based on observed needs. That small delay usually saves money and keeps your space manageable.
